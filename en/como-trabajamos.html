DayByDay Framework

Five phases from the first call to stable operation

The same process for every client, adapted in technical depth to your business.

01

Diagnosis (week 0)

30-minute call with Pablo and Jorge. Initial read of paid account, Shopify and tracking. We hand you the lever that moves the most cash in 90 days. No cost, no commitment.

02

Technical audit (weeks 1–2)

We access your advertising accounts, your store, your client list and your measurement. We review how everything is set up. We deliver a document with the plan custom-fit for your business.

03

Initial implementation (days 15–30)

We execute the first levers: account rebuild, CAPI and server-side tracking, automation of critical flows (cart, WhatsApp, CRM). Live dashboards for margin, payback and LTV.

04

Operation + scale (month 2 onwards)

Weekly meetings with Pablo and Jorge. Decisions on creatives, budget, channel expansion and automation. We scale nothing before we understand why it works.

05

Business reporting (monthly)

What is left after costs, how long it takes to recover a new client and how much they leave over time. Not platform numbers. What your leadership needs to decide on cash.

No playbooks

Ad-hoc solutions, not recycled checklists

Each client receives an SDD (System Design Document) and a PDD (Process Design Document) tailored to their stack, their team and their phase.

Agencies work with playbooks. It works when your business looks like the average. When it doesn't, they apply a template that doesn't fit and the result is noise.

We design technical infrastructure per client. If you need a conversational agent on WhatsApp for qualification, we don't send you a generic n8n workflow: Jorge designs the SDD tailored to your CRM, your volume and your fee model. If you need to rebuild the Meta account with CAPI and server-side, we don't clone another account: Pablo sets up the campaign tree that fits your channel mix and your LTV per cohort.

The cost of that is honest: we go slower in the first phase. The benefit is too: you stop paying for copy-paste.

Douf lesson

"If you capitalize on a success before understanding what produced it, you kill it."

When a creative or a channel starts to work, the reflex is to scale it. But scaling without understanding why it works destroys the result. That is why we analyze before executing, we execute before scaling, and we follow through before letting go.
